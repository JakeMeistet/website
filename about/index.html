Charity Work

As a committed individual, I have always taken pride of my 100% commitment to any task or interest I may have. An example of this is, from a very young age I have been a cycling enthusiast. In 2016, after 2 years of training, I cycled from Land’s End in Cornwall to John o’ Groats in Scotland completing a total of 999 miles in 12 consecutive days (an average of 85 miles a day). I successfully raised a total of £7298.81 money for three individual charities (Autoimmune Alopecia Research UK, Birmingham Children’s Hospital and Sandwell Young Carers).

I also set up a blog for the whole event and the training. BBC Midlands Today also featured me around the time I was starting and finishing the cycle ride. I have participated in other cycle events including 2014 Birmingham Bikeathon for leukaemia (aged 12), and cycling for men’s cancer (aged 6). I have raised money with family including Macmillan Cancer Support.

During the lockdown of COVID-19, I have also been volunteering at the local foodbank in Cannock. When I'm in I manage all the parcel requests sent in and prepare the necessary boxes for delivery. I update an Excel spreadsheet to keep track of all the parcels processed through the foodbank as well as stating when the parcels left the warehouse to be delivered. If there is less parcels in a day I also help around the warehouse, preparing packages and sorting through donations made.
